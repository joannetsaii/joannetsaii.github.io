Executive Summary

This project aims to rebuild the Health Service‚Äôs secure portal at Carnegie Mellon University. We are motivated by the flaws we discovered in the current health portal and the ambition to test new approaches and designs that are not commonly adopted such as a dark background with a lighted menu bar. Throughout the process, we focused on creating a smooth navigation experience, the aesthetic pleasure of the visual design, and intuitive feedforward through text instructions. However, we also acknowledged the challenges to satisfy simplicity and explicitness at the same time. Hence, it is crucial to seek methods that would help place the information to avoid disrupting the navigation flow.

Design Goals

Target Audience

Students and faculty who are required or would like to conduct COVID-19 testing or make an appointment for COVID-19 vaccine at Carnegie Mellon University

Goals of Expected Users
  • To understand the tabs/options when skimming through the homepage
  • To complete the process of appointment scheduling with text content that are easy to follow
  • To view immunization records and lab results in an easily-readable format
Main Considerations
  • Simplicity of Date and Time Selection: When browsing the current CMU health portal, I noticed the redundancy of date and time selections ‚Äì the available date and timeslots are listed as a long list on a single page. To reduce users‚Äô burden, the date and time selection is designed to be in separate dropdown lists.
  • Aesthetics: To prevent the pages from being filled with unnecessary information and patterns, I focused on limiting each instruction to at most two sentences and keeping the length short while indicating the most important messages. I also made sure that the main colors used, dark gray and orange, are contrasts so that the users can easily distinguish the menu bar from the main background. As for the use of images, I adjusted the saturation of the images used to make the tones more similar.
  • The Effectiveness of Instructions: Although the instructions are kept as short as possible, it is crucial that the information they convey are precise and clear to users. The instructions are written in short sentences that either start with a verb, urging the user to take certain actions, or indicate important information such as the location of COVID-19 testing. Since users would likely skim through the instructions rather than reading them carefully, I would also want to make sure that keywords are highlighted by making them bolded and underlined. For example, on the confirmation page, two important actions ‚Äì ‚Äúsave the barcode‚Äù and ‚Äúconfirm your appointment‚Äù ‚Äì are emphasized.
